## 2. What NotifyBridge does

NotifyBridge listens to Android notifications that the user explicitly grants through the system Notification Access setting. The app can filter these notifications and forward selected notification content to a destination chosen by the user, such as Bark, Telegram, Slack, or Email.

## 3. Data processed by the app

Depending on user settings, the app may process:

- Notification source app name and package name
- Notification title, text, and subtext
- Notification timestamps and metadata
- Device model and Android version
- Installed app names and package names for app-based forwarding rules
- User-provided delivery configuration such as webhook URLs, bot tokens, or SMTP settings

## 4. Why the data is processed

The app processes data to:

- Filter which notifications should be forwarded
- Show local delivery logs and retry status
- Forward selected notifications to the delivery destination configured by the user

## 5. Data sharing

When forwarding is enabled, selected notification data is transmitted to the third-party service chosen by the user. This can include:

- Bark servers
- Telegram Bot API
- Slack Incoming Webhooks
- SMTP email providers

Those third parties process data under their own privacy policies.

## 6. Local storage

The app stores settings, delivery history, and retry queue records locally on the device. Credentials used for delivery automation may also be stored locally on the device.

## 7. User control

Users can:

- Disable forwarding at any time
- Revoke Notification Access in Android settings
- Remove channel credentials
- Clear local logs from the app
